Help

You are looking at John Conway's rational tangles: the tangles you could get from two pieces of string by repeatedly picking two neighboring ends and turning them over each other.

But in this game, you have only two kinds of moves:

Z
Twist 'em up!
A
Turn 'em 'round!
Get a friend to scramble it for you, and then try untangling it. Good luck!

Spoilers

ordered by increasing spoilage

  1. One of Conway's theorems was that two rational tangles are ambient isotopic (can be turned into each other without moving or passing the ends) if and only if they represent the same fraction via some algorithm he devised.
  2. I chose to draw these tangles constrained to a dumpling surface, which dodges a lot of magic. In particular, it means the string's average slope is a fraction. So: what does each move do to the slope?
  3. To see this in action, you can make it display fractions.
  4. Mathematicians should think about (a) what has the torus as a double cover branched at the half-or-whole-integer points, and (b) what SL(2, Z) does to it.
